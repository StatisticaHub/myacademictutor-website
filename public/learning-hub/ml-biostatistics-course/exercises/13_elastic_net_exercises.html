Part G: Short written answer

Question 16

Write a short explanation of elastic net for a biomedical researcher working with omics data.

Suggested answers

Answer 1

Elastic net combines ridge and lasso. It shrinks coefficients, can perform some feature selection, and can be more stable than lasso when predictors are correlated.

Answer 2

alpha = 0   → ridge regression
alpha = 1   → lasso regression
0 < alpha < 1 → elastic net

Answer 3

lambda controls the overall strength of the regularisation penalty.

Answer 4

B. Elastic net combines ridge-like and lasso-like regularisation.

Answer 5

Situation Possible method
Many correlated predictors may each contain small predictive information Ridge or elastic net
A very small selected predictor set is desired Lasso
Predictors are highly correlated and some feature selection is desired Elastic net
Feature stability is more important than sparsity Ridge or elastic net
A compromise between shrinkage and selection is desired Elastic net

Answer 6

Elastic net includes a ridge-like component, which helps handle correlated predictors more smoothly. Lasso may choose one predictor from a correlated group and discard others, while elastic net may allow grouped predictors to contribute together.

Answer 7

Elastic net may be useful because the dataset has many predictors and many genes may be correlated through biological pathways. Elastic net can shrink coefficients, select features, and handle correlated predictors better than pure lasso.

Answer 8

No. With only four clinically meaningful predictors, ordinary logistic regression or ridge may be sufficient. Elastic net becomes more useful when there are many candidate predictors or strong correlation with a need for feature selection.

Answer 9

Neighbouring CpG sites may contain similar information. Lasso may select one CpG site and remove others, but a small change in the sample could lead to a different CpG being selected.

Answer 10

The final test set was used for model tuning. This means the test set became part of model development, so the final test AUC may be optimistic.

Answer 11

A better workflow is:

1. Split data into training and test sets.
2. Use cross-validation inside the training data to choose alpha and lambda.
3. Fit the final model on the training data.
4. Evaluate once on the untouched test set.

Answer 12

Nested cross-validation separates tuning from performance estimation. The inner loop tunes alpha and lambda. The outer loop estimates model performance. This is useful when many tuning combinations are tested.

Answer 13

No. Elastic net-selected genes are predictors in a model. They may be causal, correlated with causal factors, proxies, artefacts, or selected by chance. Causality requires additional evidence.

Answer 14

Ask:

  • Are the selected features stable?
  • Are they biologically plausible?
  • Do they validate externally?
  • Could batch effects explain them?
  • Are they measured reliably?
  • Are they available at prediction time?

Answer 15

Elastic net may improve stability, but it does not guarantee well-calibrated probabilities. Calibration must be checked because clinical decisions may depend on accurate risk estimates.

Answer 16

Elastic net is useful for omics data because it combines shrinkage and feature selection. It can handle correlated features better than lasso alone, which is helpful when genes, methylation sites, or proteins move together. However, selected features still need careful validation and cautious biological interpretation.
