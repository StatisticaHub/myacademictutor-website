About

Machine Learning in Biostatistics is a structured R-based course on machine learning for medical statistics, clinical prediction modelling, survival analysis, and biomedical data science.

The course is designed for learners who want to understand machine learning through a medical statistics and biostatistics lens, rather than only as a general data science topic.

Course aim

The aim of this course is to help learners understand how machine learning methods should be used, interpreted, validated, and reported in medical and biomedical research.

The course focuses on questions such as:

  • What is the difference between prediction and inference?
  • Why is validation important?
  • How does overfitting happen?
  • What is data leakage?
  • How should sensitivity, specificity, ROC AUC, and calibration be interpreted?
  • When is a prediction model clinically useful?
  • Why is external validation needed?
  • How can R be used to build reproducible medical prediction workflows?

Course style

This is a written, applied, R-based learning resource.

The course includes:

  • story-based lecture pages;
  • detailed theory notes;
  • practice exercises;
  • reproducible R scripts;
  • applied clinical case studies;
  • figures and interpretation.

The lectures use a conversational style with recurring teaching characters, doubts, examples, and clinical discussions. The notes remain more detailed and academic.

The goal is to connect statistical concepts with realistic medical prediction problems.

Who this course is for

This course may be useful for:

  • students in biostatistics, medical statistics, epidemiology, or health data science;
  • researchers working with biomedical or clinical data;
  • learners who know basic statistics and want to understand medical machine learning;
  • students preparing for projects involving prediction modelling, survival analysis, or biomedical data science;
  • analysts who want to improve their understanding of model validation and interpretation.
