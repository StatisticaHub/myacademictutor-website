Statistics Foundation for University Students

Course developed by My Academic Tutor.

Statistics Foundation for University Students is a theoretical and beginner-friendly course for students who want to build a strong base in statistics before studying biostatistics, epidemiology, data science, machine learning, research methods, or quantitative analysis.

The course avoids coding and focuses on concepts, notation, mathematical reasoning, derivations, interpretation, and exam-style thinking.

Learning approach

Built for understanding, not memorisation

The course is designed to help students understand why statistical methods work before using them in applied subjects.

Step 1

Understand the idea

Each lesson begins with a conversational lecture that introduces the concept using clear examples and recurring characters.

Step 2

Study the theory

Detailed notes explain notation, definitions, assumptions, derivations and common interpretation mistakes.

Step 3

Practise and check

Interactive tasks, exercises and quizzes help students test understanding before moving to the next topic.

Designed for

  • University students beginning statistics for the first time.
  • Students preparing for biostatistics, epidemiology, data science or research methods.
  • Learners who want mathematical notation explained clearly before moving to software.
  • Students who prefer conceptual, theoretical and exam-style learning without coding.

What makes it different

  • No programming distractions in the foundation stage.
  • Clear movement from definitions to interpretation.
  • Mathematical reasoning explained gradually.
  • Consistent structure across every lesson.
  • Useful preparation for applied quantitative subjects.
