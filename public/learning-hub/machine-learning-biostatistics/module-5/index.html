Module 5

Tree-Based Methods

This module explains decision trees, random forests, gradient boosting, XGBoost, variable importance and partial dependence for medical prediction.

Start Module 5 Back to Module 4

Module aim

Understand flexible prediction without losing clinical caution.

Tree-based methods can model non-linear effects and interactions without requiring the analyst to specify them manually. They are powerful, but they can also overfit, hide important modelling choices, and produce misleading interpretations if they are not validated carefully.

Key ideas

What you will learn to judge.

Non-linearity and interactions

Tree-based methods can learn patterns that ordinary linear models may miss.

Overfitting and tuning

Flexible models require careful control of depth, number of trees, learning rate and validation design.

Interpretation with caution

Variable importance and partial dependence help interpretation, but they do not prove causality.
